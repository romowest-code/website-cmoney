How to Choose a Contractor Business Name

What Makes a Great Business Name

  • Easy to spell and pronounce
  • Memorable and unique
  • Conveys professionalism and trust
  • Fits on a truck, business card, and uniform
  • Available as a domain name and LLC

Common Naming Patterns for Contractors

  1. Name + Trade: "Johnson Plumbing"
  2. Location + Trade: "Denver Electric"
  3. Quality Words: "Precision", "Pro", "Master", "Elite"
  4. Wordplay: "Watt's Up Electric", "Drain Surgeons"

Names to Avoid

  • Hard to spell names
  • Generic names with numbers (AAA Handyman, 123 Plumbing)
  • Names that limit growth (if you do more than one trade)
  • Names too similar to competitors

Frequently Asked Questions

Should I use my own name in my business name?

Using your name builds personal brand equity but can make the business harder to sell later. "Mike's Handyman" vs "Precision Home Services"—one is about you, one is about the service.

Do I need to check if the name is available?

Yes—check your state's business registry, trademark database, and domain availability before committing.

Should my business name include my location?

Location names work well for local SEO ("Denver Plumbing") but limit expansion. Consider if you might serve multiple cities.
